Chapter 9: Building Your Acquisition Team

Home / Creative Acquisitions / Chapter 9

Acquisition success depends on assembling the right team around you. Chapter 9 explores how to build, manage, and leverage specialized advisors who enhance your deal-making capability. The best acquisitions strategists are rarely lone wolves -- they're brilliant orchestrators of talent.

Lawyers: Your Deal Architecture Experts

You need attorneys who specialize in acquisitions, understand the tax implications of deal structure, and recognize subtle liability issues others miss. A good acquisition lawyer doesn't just review documents -- they help you negotiate better terms and identify hidden risks.

Brokers and Dealers: Your Deal Pipeline

Quality brokers provide more than listings -- they provide market intelligence, off-market access, and buyer-seller matchmaking. When you have strong relationships with respected brokers, they bring deals to you before they hit the market.

Building Relationship Architecture

The power of your acquisition team emerges when they communicate with each other. Your lawyer should discuss structure with your lender. Your advisor should validate assumptions with your broker. When information flows freely and perspectives intersect, you catch problems and discover opportunities.

Key Takeaways

  • Hire acquisition specialists, not generalists -- brokers, lawyers, and advisors focused on your asset class
  • Relationships with brokers compound -- early deals lead to off-market access
  • Lenders are partners, not just capital sources -- develop relationships before you need money
  • Coordinate your team -- communication across advisors catches problems sooner
  • Invest in trust -- teams that close the best deals operate as coordinated units
"Your team determines your ceiling. Surround yourself with experts who make you sharper, not just busier."
