The 7 Minute Phone Call

Chapter 7: Voicemail and Gatekeeper Strategies

Home / The 7 Minute Phone Call / Chapter 7

The voicemail sits in your prospect's inbox, and gatekeeper assistants control access to decision-makers. Chapter 7 reveals the tactical strategies that bypass these obstacles and ensure your message gets through.

The Voicemail That Gets Returned

Most voicemails fail immediately. They meander, lack urgency, or bore the listener into deletion. A voicemail that gets returned does three things: establishes credibility, creates curiosity, and provides a simple reason to call back. Structure your voicemail tightly and avoid leaving your entire pitch on voicemail.

Getting Past the Gatekeeper

An assistant's job is managing their boss's time. When you approach gatekeepers as obstacles, they sense it and block harder. When you approach them as allies to their boss's success, they help. Treat gatekeepers with genuine respect and get their name.

The Warm Introduction

Getting introduced by someone the prospect trusts removes the gatekeeper problem entirely. When you mention the referrer early, the conversation tone shifts immediately. Always ask satisfied customers or contacts for introductions.

Key Takeaways

  • Voicemails that get returned establish credibility and create curiosity
  • Treat gatekeepers as allies, not obstacles
  • Break patterns with timing and channel variety -- early morning calls reach decision-makers directly
  • Warm introductions bypass gatekeepers entirely
  • Email-first approach followed by voicemail creates context
"Gatekeepers aren't obstacles -- they're gatekeepers because they protect something valuable. Treat them with respect and they become your allies."
